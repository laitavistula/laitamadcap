Changes in didactics

Spearheaded by the digital revolution, the substantial impact of the DVD's emergence in the mid-1990s and new legislative measures promoting media communication access led to the creation of a range of higher education programs in AVT across Europe at the turn of the millennium. This expansion coincided with the graduation of the first generation of AVT trainers, whose doctoral theses were completed and defended during the 1990s. Additionally, associationslike ESIST founded in 1995, were established to facilitate information exchange and promote professional standards in screen translation training and practice.

ESIST, which encompasses higher education teachers, practitioners, academics, and students in AVT, has played a key role in raising awareness, creating visibility, and fostering synergies among stakeholders.

The main aims of ESIST are to:

  • Facilitate information and experience exchange in the teaching of audiovisual translation at European higher education institutions..

  • Promote audiovisual translation as a skilled practice that requires specialist training, encouraging the creation of innovative screen translation courses in European institutions.

  • Facilitate cooperative projects, including the exchange of staff and students.

  • Study the potential for curriculum development in the field of audiovisual translation on a European basis.

  • Initiate other forms of collaboration and research in audiovisual translation.

  • Enhance dialogue between higher education instructors of AVT and professionals in the television and film industries.

  • Promote professional standards in the teaching and practice of audiovisual translation.

  • Advocate for accessibility to audiovisual media.
