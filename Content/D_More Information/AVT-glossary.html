Glossary of Audiovisual Translation (AVT) Terms

Accessibility
The practice of ensuring audiovisual content is usable by individuals with disabilities, including subtitles for the deaf and hard of hearing (SDH) and audio description (AD).
Audio Description (AD)
A narrated track that describes visual elements of a video, designed for visually impaired audiences.
Cloud Computing
A technology enabling remote storage and access to data and tools, widely used in AVT for subtitling and dubbing workflows.
Interlingual Subtitling
The translation of spoken or written content from one language to another in the form of on-screen text.
Intralingual Subtitling
The transcription of spoken content into text within the same language, often used for SDH or language learning purposes.
Machine Translation (MT)
Automated translation produced by algorithms, often used in AVT workflows with post-editing to refine results.
Post-Editing (PE)
The process of revising and refining machine-translated content to improve accuracy and readability.
Respeaking
A technique where a speaker dictates live subtitles using ASR (automatic speech recognition) technology, commonly used in live broadcasting.
Subtitles for the Deaf and Hard of Hearing (SDH)
Subtitles designed to convey dialogue, sound effects, and other auditory information to viewers with hearing impairments.
Template
A timecoded script used as a reference for creating subtitles in multiple languages, streamlining the subtitling process.
