Pre-editing

Pre-editing is the process in which a human prepares a document before applying machine translation. The primary goal of pre-editing is to reduce the post-editing workload by adapting the source document to improve the quality of the machine translation output. Pre-editing can also be valuable for human translation projects, as it enhances the effectiveness of translation memory.

In general, pre-editing is worth applying when there are more than three target languages. In this case, pre-editing should facilitate the process of machine translation by providing:

  • spell and grammar checking

  • avoiding complex or ambiguous syntactic structure

  • verifying term consistency

However, it is also applicable to poorly-converted files. Linguistic pre-editing is more important than pre-editing of the format since errors can affect machine translation quality.
